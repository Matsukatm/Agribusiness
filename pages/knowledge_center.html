Pest Management Solutions

Identify and treat common garden pests using organic, locally available solutions

Aphids

Common on sukuma wiki, tomatoes

Identification:

Small green/black insects clustering on leaves

Organic Solution:

Soap spray: 2 tbsp dish soap + 1L water

Prevention:

Plant marigolds nearby, regular inspection

Cutworms

Attack young seedlings at soil level

Identification:

Seedlings cut at base, brown caterpillars in soil

Organic Solution:

Collar protection with cardboard tubes

Prevention:

Clear weeds, hand-pick at night

Whiteflies

Common on tomatoes, capsicum

Identification:

Tiny white flying insects under leaves

Organic Solution:

Yellow sticky traps, neem oil spray

Prevention:

Good air circulation, companion planting

My tomato leaves are turning yellow. What could be wrong?

I planted tomatoes 6 weeks ago and they were doing well, but now the lower leaves are turning yellow and dropping. The plants are in containers on my balcony.

John Mwangi Certified Expert

This is likely nitrogen deficiency, common in container plants. The yellowing starts from bottom leaves as the plant moves nutrients to new growth. Try liquid fertilizer every 2 weeks and ensure good drainage. Also check if containers are large enough - tomatoes need at least 20L containers.
